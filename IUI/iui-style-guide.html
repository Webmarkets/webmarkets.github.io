IUI Brand Guide

A comprehensive guide for maintaining brand consistency and incorporating creative identity into all Idaho Urology Institute projects for 2026.

Brand Guideline Purpose

This guide serves as the source of truth for how IUI shows up visually — ensuring every patient touchpoint reflects the practice's authority, compassion, and clinical excellence.

01

Brand Personality

CLINICAL COMPASSIONATE TRUSTWORTHY PRECISE ACCESSIBLE AUTHORITATIVE PATIENT-FIRST

Tone In Practice

✓ Calm, confident, reassuring — never clinical to the point of cold
✓ Plain language first — jargon only when it builds authority
✓ Lead with the patient outcome, not the procedure
✓ Numbers and credentials reinforce trust — use them

Color Palette

Primary Gradient

Blue → Navy

Hero sections, CTAs, gates

On Light Backgrounds

Use IUI Blue for links, buttons, and icons. Use Deep Navy for all headline text.

On Dark Backgrounds

Use IUI Blue for accent elements only. White for all body and headline text.

On Blue Backgrounds

White for all text. Use sparingly — reserve gradient for hero and CTA blocks.

03

Typography

Headlines & Body — Inter

Expert Urological Care
Across Idaho.

Inter is our primary typeface for all patient-facing and marketing materials. Clean, highly readable across all sizes, and professional without being cold. Used for both headlines and body copy to maintain a modern clinical aesthetic.

300 Light 400 Regular 500 Medium 600 Semibold 700 Bold 800 Extrabold ← headlines

Eyebrows & Labels — Barlow Condensed

PRECISION CARE · COMPASSIONATE OUTCOMES

Barlow Condensed is used for section eyebrows, navigation labels, service tags, and any short uppercase callout text. It carries authority without bulk — keeping the layout clean while adding structure.

Logo Usage Rules

✓ Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the "I" on all sides
✓ Minimum display size: 120px wide for digital, 1 inch for print
✕ Do not stretch, rotate, or alter logo proportions
✕ Do not place on busy photography without a protective overlay

06

Imagery Direction

Use This

✓ Warm, natural lighting — clean clinical environments without sterile coldness
✓ Physicians in white coats or scrubs — approachable, direct eye contact
✓ Diverse patient demographics — men, women, all ages represented
✓ Idaho landscapes and community imagery for location pages
✓ Modern, clean facility photography that communicates quality of care

Avoid This

✕ Generic stock imagery with over-posed or obviously fake interactions
✕ Graphic medical imagery — keep all visuals patient-safe and approachable
✕ Cold, harsh lighting that makes facilities feel intimidating
✕ Imagery with unrelated colors that fight the IUI blue and navy palette
✕ Outdated or low-resolution photography — quality signals clinical quality
