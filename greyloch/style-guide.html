04

The Arch

The barrel vault roof of our Meridian headquarters is a defining architectural feature — a sweeping curve that symbolizes strength, ambition, and forward momentum. This arc is a core design motif referenced throughout digital and print work.

Barrel Vault Arc — the curve that defines us

Usage Guidelines

  • Use as a decorative hero element on landing pages
  • Subtle section dividers with low-opacity arcs
  • Structural ribs suggest the building's steel frame
  • Always pair with brand green (#009639)
  • Fill opacity: 0.04–0.08; stroke opacity: 0.35–0.55

SVG Curve Spec

M 0 H Q (W/2) (H × 0.12) W H stroke: #009639 stroke-opacity: 0.35–0.55 stroke-width: 1–2px fill: rgba(0,150,57, 0.04–0.08)
05

Window Grid

The curtain wall glass facade of our building — floor-to-ceiling glass panels divided by dark metal mullions — is our signature texture. This glass grid is used as a translucent panel treatment across all digital touchpoints to ground design in the architecture.

GLASS FACADE TEXTURE

Window grid — 96px × 88px

CSS Spec

background-image: linear-gradient( rgba(255,255,255,0.034) 1px, transparent 1px ), linear-gradient(90deg, rgba(255,255,255,0.034) 1px, transparent 1px ); background-size: 96px 88px;

Panel Properties

  • Background: rgba(44, 48, 44, 0.22)
  • Backdrop filter: blur(14px)
  • Border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.07)
  • Grid line opacity: 0.03–0.06
  • Grid size: 80–120px × 64–100px
  • Border radius: 2px (structural)

Corner Radius

Greyloch's architecture is defined by structural precision — clean angles with minimal softening. Our corner radius system mirrors the building's character: nearly sharp for major containers, with minimal rounding on interactive elements.
